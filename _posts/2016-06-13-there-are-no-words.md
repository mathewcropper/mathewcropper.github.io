*I'm not sure where this post is going to go, but I feel the need to express myself somehow. Please forgive my rambling and repetition.*

The [events of recent days](http://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-36492127) have left me feeling somewhat numb, and angry, and upset. A terrible thing has happened, not just to our community, but to some of the most vulnerable memebers of it.

I am a cis, white, English speaking, middle-class male. I am not actively involved in the LGBTQ+ community. I do not know the people who fell victim to what happened, and I can't claim to be close to anyone in my own life who faces the same challenges. I am not qualified to have any great opinion or authority on the subject, and I hope that this post doesn't give any impression otherwise.

I think, all I really want to say is that this was a wake up call. It was a wake up call to me that I don't do enough. I don't care enough. I don't speak out enough. By my inaction, I am complicit in the events that haunt and stalk us.

Someone shouts at me in the street, and I ignore it. Someone runs their finger across their throat when I show affection to my partner, and I brush it off. I look around and think twice about touching or holding the hand of the man I love, and I choose inaffection instead of love.

It took the death of fifty people to make me see how all of these small things add up together.

Let's not forget, this is not about me. This is about the poor people who fell victim to a society that tolerates homophobia, biphobia, transphobia (all the phobias). This is also about us grieving as a community, and standing up together to say that this is not right. And, in my own small corner of the world, it's about me realising that I have to do something small to make the world a better place around me.

End of rant.
