## <a name="leading-by-influence"></a>Leading by influence

I've never got on with "I say, you do" bosses. We learn by doing, we find reward in responsibility, and there are better ways to help people grow. I believe strongly that the best leaders are influencers of people, not managers. With that must come some awareness of self and what works for others.

Here are three excellent pieces, all by VPs at Facebook, sharing the things they learned about unconventional leadership and leading by influence.

* [Julie Zhuo - Unintuitive things I've learned about management (part 1)](https://medium.com/the-year-of-the-looking-glass/unintuitive-things-i-ve-learned-about-management-f2c42d68604b#.d6p1sqsb5)

## <a name="role-of-the-product-manager"></a>The role of the Product Manager

In August the question that has most been on my mind is, "Am I a good Product Manager?". You might very well be asking yourself the same thing. Consequently, I have spent a lot of time reading about what PM roles look like elsewhere, and what's expected there.

[John Cutler](https://twitter.com/johncutlefish), who is well worth a follow on Twitter and Medium, wrote an excellent piece entitled [The Evolving Product Manager Role](https://medium.com/@johnpcutler/the-evolving-product-manager-role-6f288bbc3cda). In it, he emphasises the role of the Product Manager as a strong and conscientious facilitator. A vehicle for empowering people. An outcome focused individual.

On the _Mind the Product_ blog, Fred Esere wrote [Product Rockstars have Head, Hands, and Heart](http://www.mindtheproduct.com/2016/08/product-rockstars-have-head-hands-and-heart/), where he emphasised the strategic and intellectual disciplines more. Reading between the lines, the Product Manager here is someone who is a leader who doesn't mind getting dirty in the trenches every now and then.

Ken Norton's post, [How to Hire a Product Manager](https://www.kennorton.com/essays/productmanager.html), describes someone who is a mixture of both. An accomplished leader, facilitator, with insight and technical skills to succeed.

It goes to show that the discipine, if you can call it that, of Product Management is very much evolving. There isn't a single benchmark, but there are people out there who have been successful. They have been successful in myriad ways. Perhaps it's better not to worry so much about benchmarking myself against others, and instead focus on carving out our own path.

## <a name="gif-workflows"></a>Gif workflows

The rise of the gif! However you pronounce it, you can't get away from them. Aside from being a lot of fun (I have a Ryan Gosling gif for all occasions), they have actually turned out to be a super useful tool for actual, real work things!

Srsly.

Zendesk tickets support markdown, which means I can easily embed a gif in a reply to a customer. "How do I.." questions are now really easily answered, with some text and a well placed gif or two.

My preferred workflow for creating gifs is:

* Record whatever it is you're showing off using QuickTime Player. I like having a high fidelity video in case I need it again later.
* Use [Gif Brewery 3](https://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/gif-brewery-3-video-to-gif/id1081413713?mt=12) to turn the selected parts of the recording into a gif.
* If I need to host the gif, it goes into my public [Dropbox](https://www.dropbox.com) folder.

9 times out of 10, that's what I do. I have tried other tools:

* [Cloud App](https://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/cloudapp/id417602904?mt=12) is great, but I stopped using it just because it was expensive for how I used it. Aside from the price, this was fantastic.
* [Gyazo](https://gyazo.com) is one I tried some time ago. It was nice, but not quite what I needed. Since then, it's come on leaps and bounds, and I'd certainly recommend it as an alternative to Cloud App.
* [LICEcap](http://www.cockos.com/licecap/) is a very lightweight way to record gifs directly from your screen. I moved away from this as I wanted to keep high fidelity videos _just in case_.
* [Gifox](https://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/gifox/id1082624744?mt=12) is one I tried but can't recommend. I found it to be slow to take a screen recording and output the gif. Occasionally, I had to re-record gifs due to long stretches of dropped frames.
* If you want to create a gif just for fun, [Giphy's own gif creator web app](https://giphy.com/create/gifmaker) is the place to go.
