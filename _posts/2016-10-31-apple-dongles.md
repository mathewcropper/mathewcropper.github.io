## Embracing wireless

Don't get me wrong, there are some down sides to this new setup, but it isn't the end of the world. That photo is crafted for dramatic effect when, in fact, Apple's intention is something much closer to this. Notice the lack of wires.

![](/images/posts/20161031_wireless.jpg)
*iPhone 7 plus, [Beats Powerbeats2](https://www.cnet.com/uk/products/beats-powerbeats2-wireless/review/), 12 inch Macbook (early 2015)*

This is my current setup, and it works just as nicely for me today as it did before USB-C-gate or headphone-gate. It does 'just work'. To make it work required one change for me; a commitment to ditching the cables and going wireless.

Here are the things I can do:

* I can listen to music whilst charging my phone. Wired charger + wireless headphones.
* I can back up my phone to my Macbook. iTunes has supported wireless backups for years.
* I can use an external display with my Macbook, and still type and use a mouse. That's right.. wirelss keyboard and mouse. In fact, the **only** dongle I own is a USB-C <> HMDI adaptor for this purpose.

## Something to legitimately gripe about

If you don't own all of these wireless things, then this setup is gonna suck for a while until you do. You will need a few dongles. When you have a wireless setup that you like, it's all seamless, though.

If you haven't been heading in a wireless direction already, there's a cost associated with going wireless now. You can legitimately gripe about that. It's not exactly cheap, although there are options at both ends of the cost scale.

Battery life on some of your wireless devices may not be that great, but now that Apple has shifted in this direction we are bound to see advances in battery life from accessory manufacturers.

There are some things to be annoyed about, for sure, but let's not blow the roof off here. This is a change, and it comes with an adjustment period, but I firmly believe that a wireless future is something we can get behind.
