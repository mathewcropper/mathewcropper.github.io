Most of the team has taken the last couple of weeks off, enjoying a well deserved break. Maybe your customers have as well. Getting together again is going to be awesome, and there's going to be a lot of energy. Here's my post-holiday checklist for making the most of that, channelling it into reconnecting with our shared goals and direction.

## There are two teams; the pigs and the chickens

![The story of the chicken and the pig](/images/posts/20170102-chicken-pig.png)

In the story of the pig and the chicken, the two set off on a business idea to open a restaurant. It becomes apparent very quickly that, between them, very different levels of commitment are required.

I like to think in terms of pigs and chickens when thinking of the 'return to work' problem. The pigs are the engineers, designers, and product team who are at the coal face day to day. The chickens are the people who care about what the pigs are doing, but aren't deep in the detail.

When we come back to work after the December break, it's important to remember that the pigs aren't the only ones who need to reconnect, it's the chickens as well.

## The pigs

The team(s) returning to building the product will have the most energy. Make the most of that by reinforcing their enthusiasm and directing their attention to a positive future.

Here's my playbook for doing this effectively every time:

* **Look back on previous successes:** have a thoughtful and attractive email sitting at the top of everyone's inbox on the first day back, summarising the previous year's successes. Take inspiration from [Algolia's year in review](https://www.algolia.com/2016) or [Medium's 2016 compendium](https://medium.com/me/stats/summary/2016-154ac95683af). Use a free [MailChimp](https://mailchimp.com) account to build an attractive email very quickly.
* **Make the start of the day social:** The first few hours of the day are all about reconnecting with one another. Clear the morning of meetings, put out some pastries, and just spend time talking. Bonus points if you can convince people to take a team photo to start the year ;)
* **Have a short team meeting:** Start the meeting by reiterating the previous year's successes to reinforce the positive mood. If you have work already in flight, then spend some time talking about it. This will start to get everyone's thinking back onto the work.
  * What's the problem or user need we're solving?
  * Where did we get to? What's in progress right now?
  * What's happening this week/sprint? Do we have what we need to get going again?

## The chickens

After a break, your chickens will feel the most disconnected. Here's how to keep them up to date and feeling involved.

* **Look back on previous successes as well:** Chickens should get the same email on day one as the pigs. We're in it together, and our successes are shared.
* **Set aside 30 minutes to talk:** Make sure that in the first week back you have coffee (if you're in the same location), or set up a video call, with your chickens. Yes, it'll take a chunk of time out of your week, but it will ensure that people feel included and part of the team. It'll also stop people feeling uncomfortable and having to chase you to get updates.

## Your customers

Don't forget to re-establish a connection with your customers. In January, try to have a call with at least one customer each week. It's a month for retrospectives and introspection, so I guarantee they'll have something to say. [Jack Dorsey did this via Twitter](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-12-30/twitter-ceo-solicits-product-feedback-as-he-takes-larger-role), asking for feedback on both Twitter and Square.

_Pro tip: you should be doing this every week anyway. Why not make this a regular habit?_

Just catch up, don't necessarily set an agenda, but try to understand a few things:

* What are their biggest goals or needs right now, even if they're nothing to do with your product?
* What didn't work for them last year [with your product]?
* If you can share what you're working on now, do so, and hear what they have to say.

Make sure you record these sessions, or take detailed notes, and share them with everyone in your team. These are valuable insights.

When you're done, ask yourself what you learned from the experience and what actions you need to take.

## Yourself

Set aside some time to [think about your career goals](http://www.mathewcropper.co.uk/techniques/2016/12/27/manage-your-career.html) and personal development. What's **your** direction? What do you want to develop or learn? What's on your reading list?

Do this to reconnect with yourself as a Product Manager. If you followed this playbook, you just spent a lot of time thinking about a lot of other people. You owe yourself at least a few minutes.
